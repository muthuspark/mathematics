sin θ = 1/csc θ and csc θ = 1/sin θ

Trignometry
Reciprocal Identities

To prove that \(\sin \theta = \frac{1}{\csc \theta}\)and\(\csc \theta = \frac{1}{\sin \theta}\), let’s proceed step by step.

Step 1: Understand the Definitions

  • Sine Function (\(\sin \theta\)): In a right triangle, it represents the ratio of the length of the side opposite to an angle θ (relative to the hypotenuse).

  • Cosecant Function (\(\csc \theta\)): It is defined as the reciprocal of the sine function. Specifically: \[\csc \theta = \frac{1}{\sin \theta}\]Step 2: Prove\(\sin \theta = \frac{1}{\csc \theta}\)

  • Starting with the definition of cosecant: \[\csc \theta = \frac{1}{\sin \theta}\]- To find\(\sin \theta\)in terms of\(\csc \theta\), take the reciprocal of both sides: \[\sin \theta = \frac{1}{\csc \theta}\]Conclusion:\[\sin \theta = \frac{1}{\csc \theta} \quad \text{and} \quad \csc \theta = \frac{1}{\sin \theta}\]

This completes the proof of both equalities.
