#Trignometry #Double Angle Formulas

To prove the trigonometric identities for cos(2θ), we can follow these steps:

  1. \[ \cos(A + B) = \cos A \cos B - \sin A \sin B \]
  2. \[ \cos(θ + θ) = \cos²θ - sin²θ \]
  3. \[ \cos(2θ) = cos²θ - sin²θ \]

  4. Express in terms of power-reduction formulas:

    • From \( \sin²θ + cos²θ = 1 \), solve for cos²θ: \[ cos²θ = 1 - sin²θ \]
    • Substitute into the double-angle formula: \[ cos(2θ) = (1 - sin²θ) - sin²θ = 1 - 2sin²θ \]
  5. \[ sin²θ = 1 - cos²θ \]
  6. \[ cos(2θ) = cos²θ - (1 - cos²θ) = 2cos²θ - 1 \]

Thus, we have derived all three forms of the double-angle identity for cosine:

\[ cos(2θ) = cos²θ - sin²θ = 1 - 2sin²θ = 2cos²θ - 1 \]
